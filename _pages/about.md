I am a **Ph.D. candidate in Automatic Control** and an **R&D engineer in robotics, control, and autonomous systems**. My background combines industrial R&D experience at **Stellantis** with hands-on projects in **UAV control, robotics, embedded systems, and prototyping**.

I currently work on **autonomous driving control systems** in an industrial R&D environment while pursuing doctoral research on **decision and control systems for autonomous vehicles**.

## Background

- **INSA Lyon** — MEng in Mechanical Engineering, specialization in **Mechatronics and Systems**.
- **EPFL** — Exchange semester in Mechanical Engineering, with coursework in **mobile robotics, robotics for manipulation, nonlinear control, multivariable control, and embedded motor control**.
- **IRIMAS – Université de Haute-Alsace** — Ph.D. in **Automatic Control** (2024–2027), focused on decision and control systems for autonomous vehicles.
- **Stellantis** — Current industrial R&D role in **autonomous driving** and previous internship on **digital twin-based load identification**.

## Current Work

My current work lies at the intersection of **autonomous driving**, **decision and control systems**, and **industrial R&D**.

- At **Stellantis**, I contribute to the development of **control algorithms for autonomous driving and ADAS**, with applications to **vehicle dynamics and motion control**.
- My doctoral research focuses on **multi-controller architectures for automated driving in heterogeneous and critical scenarios**.
- More broadly, I am interested in the development of reliable autonomous systems that combine **modeling, simulation, control, estimation, and experimental validation**.

## Research & Engineering Interests

My main research and engineering interests include:

These interests are reflected both in my industrial work and in my projects, including **autonomous driving control**, **quadcopter stabilization**, **robot localization and path planning**, and **CubeSat embedded electronics**.
